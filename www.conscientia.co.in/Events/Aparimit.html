Astroreflection


“Who are we? We find that we live on an insignificant planet of a humdrum star lost in a galaxy tucked away in some forgotten corner of a universe in which there are far more galaxies than people.” – Carl Sagan

We now know that these asterisks are huge spheres of burning hydrogen. But what happens when the Hydrogen runs out, if it ever does? Are all the white speckles we see in the sky stars? If not, what exactly are they?

We expect you to answer some of these questions and some questions of your own. The only condition is to make a poster to display that information. So, pick up your pen and/or your phone and start thinking of a topic, (two topics are just as fine) and create an attractive technical poster out of your idea.



Format

  1. The event consists of 2 rounds.
  2. All the participating teams have to submit an abstract describing the poster. It must be simple and informative. The appropriate references must also be mentioned.
  3. Based on the abstract few teams will be selected to the final round and they will be notified.
  4. Posters will be displayed during the main event and the teams will have to present their poster before the judges.
  5. Teams will be judged based on their poster presentation, information about the topic, posters content and their explanatory skills.
  6. The charges incurred to print the posters have to be arranged by the participants themselves.

Prize Money

₹ 5,500

x

Astroreflection



Rules

  1. This event is open to all college students with valid ID cards.
  2. A team may consist of a maximum of 2 members.
  3. The poster topics must be related to astronomy or cosmology.
  4. A team can submit a maximum of two posters.
  5. Poster should be in .png or .jpeg format.
  6. Posters can be made on an A1 sheet or alternatively you can use either two A2 sheets.
  7. Text size must be chosen such that it is readable from a comfortable distance.
  8. The decision of the event organizers will be final and binding under all circumstances.

Night Sky Hunt


"Though my soul may set in darkness, it will rise in perfect light; I have loved the stars too fondly to be fearful of the night." - Sarah Williams

The night sky has been a host for many mysterious objects. Thanks to pioneers like Galileo and his modified telescope, the quest for these mysterious objects has been made easier and since then the hunt is unending. Be a part of this hunt and grab your opportunity to find your way through the night sky.

Prize Money

₹ 6,000

x

Night Sky Hunt



Rules

  1. Maximum two members are allowed in a team.
  2. A maximum of 2 participants in a team can register.
  3. The event will consist of two rounds: prelims and finals.
  4. Prelims will be a written round to test the knowledge of participants in observational astronomy and telescopes.
  5. Finals will test the ability to identify astronomical objects by using telescopes or naked eye.
  6. The participants will be given 90 minutes and objects to be identified may include Messier objects, planets, stars, sky patterns etc.
  7. The names of objects may be given directly or the participants will have to find it from the hints provided.
  8. Telescopes, Star charts and all the required materials will be provided by the organizing team.
  9. Rough handling of telescopes or damages to its components may lead to disqualification and fine.
  10. Cheating will not be tolerated. Hence use of mobile phones, laptops, own star charts, books and so on are condemned.
  11. Decision of the organizers will be final.
  12. There will initially be a written elimination round, with the selected teams getting to participate in the finals.
  13. Within the given time the participants have to identify maximum of the given objects.
  14. The accuracy of identification of objects and the knowledge about them will be also considered as evaluation criteria.
