Machinist


Does the sound of metal meeting metal excite you? Do you find the sparks of welding riveting? Here is a platform for you to test you machinist skills.



Format

  1. It consists of 2 rounds.
  2. 1st round will be a written prelim in which questions on various basic manufacturing processes, theory of machines, engineering graphics will be asked.
  3. 2nd round will be a practical round in which, the participants will be given a drawing of the component to be made and they need to make the component on the given machine.

Prize Money

₹ 8,500

x

Machinist



Rules

  1. Team should consist of maximum 2 members.
  2. The team members can be from different colleges.
  3. Participants must stick to the guidelines given.
  4. Participants must take care that their activity does not physically harm other participants in the Hall.
  5. Use of any material not provided by the organizers is strictly prohibited. Participants doing so will be disqualified.
  6. Scoring patterns will be mentioned at the time of the event
  7. This event is open to all college students with valid ID cards.
  8. The decision of the event organizers will be final and binding under all circumstances.


Evaluation

  1. 1st round: Each question will carry some marks and based on the maximum marks obtained, the top 5 teams will be selected for the 2nd round.
  2. 2nd round: Based on quality and accuracy of the component made, marks will be awarded.

Screwed


Basically an Assemble-Disassemble game in which you have to break your head to survive. Conscientia 2018 gives you the opportunity to play with screws , drivers and many other tools. So get up and get ready to face the challenge and to unscrew the Screwed.



Format

  1. This event comprises of three rounds.
  2. This is an individual event.
  3. The participant has to pick up a chit from a lot by which the participant will be assigned a device.
  4. The first round is an elimination round, in the next rounds the participant has to find number 1 to 5 that are hidden at different places inside the device, the participants have to find any three parts at level 2,all five at level 3 and in addition to disassembling, the participant has to identify a working mechanism.
  5. For every 15 participants in the first round, 5 participants will be advanced to the next round, and out of that five 3 will advance to round 3.
  6. The third round will be conducted between all the participants that will reach third round and a winner will be selected.
  7. For finding the given parts or hints, they’ll have to disassemble the device properly, and search with keen observation, thoroughness.
  8. After finding the three/five spots, they’ll have to reassemble the device back perfectly, failing which will disqualify them.
  9. Use of unfair means is strictly prohibited and this will lead to disqualification of the participant from the event.


Evaluation

  1. In the first round after the participant is allotted a device he will be given 3 minutes time for disassembly and 3 minutes to re-assemble the device back.
  2. In the first round for every part the participant disassembles he/she will be given 5 points, if the participant fails to reassemble the given parts in given 3 minutes of time and takes extra time for every 30 seconds 5 points will be deducted; in case if the participant wants to stop reassembly then for each part he/she did not reassemble 3 points will be deducted.
  3. In round 2 the participant will be given 5 minutes time to find the 3 numbers given to him/her and 3 minutes to reassemble the parts back, for every correct number the participant finds he will be awarded 5 points; if the participant fails to reassemble all the parts in 3 min and takes extra time for every 30 seconds 5 points will be deducted and in case the participant chooses to stop after 3 minutes without reassembling all the parts 3 points per part will be deducted.
  4. In round 3 the participant will be given 5 minutes to find all the 5 five numbers, for every number he/she gets 5 points, they will be given 3 minutes of reassembly time and for every 30 seconds extra time taken 5 points will be deducted, in caseif the participant wishes to stop reassembly for each part he/she fails to reassemble 3 points will be deducted.

Prize Money

₹ 6,500

  2. In case of a tie levels, participant with least time in completing the task shall be declared as the winner.
  3. After finding every number/clue/answer, the participant must report to the concerned volunteer/organizer.
  4. Decision of organizers shall be final and binding.

Contraption


There’s nothing like the satisfaction of a plan falling into place. “Contraptions” is your chance to invoke your creativity and come up with your own grand plan to build a Rube Goldberg machine



Format

  1. The final task is to spread honey over a slice of bread and cover it up with another slice of bread. The bread and the honey will be provided on the spot. Participants are required to bring ALL the materials required for building their contraption other than the materials provided by the organisers.
  2. A preparation time of 3 hrs will be given and run time should not exceed 7 minutes including the final action.
  3. A maximum space of 10ftX10ft will be provided per team.
  4. One power plug point will be provided per team.
  5. In case of a tie, the contraption with innovative ideas and more number of steps in the given time will be considered as a winner.
  6. Maximum of 2 trials will be allowed per team out of which the best will be chosen.
  7. Teams will have 3 minutes before the first run to explain their contraption.
  8. In case a contraption gets stuck during the run, then with the permission of the Judge(s), one of the participants will be allowed to intervene (with a penalty).
  9. Explosives and hazardous materials are STRICTLY NOT ALLOWED.
  10. Any intentional or unintentional damage to other team's machine will result in an immediate disqualification.
  11. Decision of the Judges will be final and binding

Prize Money

₹ 16,000

x

Contraption



Rules

  1. Run-time will not be a factor in the Evaluation.
  2. Final score will be decided entirely by the Judges based on the following criteria. INNOVATION (20%)
  3. Entirely upto the judges to decide qualitatively. Depending on ingenuity of the Design. NUMBER OF STEPS (40%)
  4. A step is defined as anything which starts with a trigger (eg : Motion Transfer, Energy Transfer, Chemical Reactions) from a previous step and results in an action. Similar or identical steps will be counted as one.(eg : 1000 falling dominos on a table will be counted as a single step). PARALLEL ROUTES (5%)
  5. Step which has actions occurring in parallel and ends in the same trigger. PERFECT RUN(No Interventions) (20%)
  6. First Intervention will reduce 5% and every subsequent one will reduce 2% each. FINAL ACTION OF MACHINE(10%)
  7. 10% marks will be allotted for completion of the task that is given. AESTHETICALLY PLEASING DESIGNS (5%)
  8. Qualitatively decided by the judges based on the best usage of the materials.

Junkyard Wars


Are your thoughts creative enough to create wonders? If yes, then this is the right platform for you to show off your caliber and skills! Nothing is a waste in the eyes of a mechanical engineer! Utilize anything and everything in the junkyard to succeed in your mission. Learn to improvise and innovate to beat the other teams. There will be three rounds to test your creativity and skills. Additional rules will be provided at the beginning of each round. So buckle up for a thrill ride!

Prize Money

₹ 27,000

x

Junkyard Wars



Format

  1. Each team consists of maximum of 5 members. The event consists of three rounds:

      2. Part 1

    1. The prelims paper will be theory-based testing the general aptitude of the teams (some of them would challenge you technically).
    2. Questions will be based on logical reasoning and some practical problems.
    3. The selection of the teams will be based on the marks scored in the test.

      Part 2

    1. This round is an application and time-based round which would definitely test your mechanical creativity and improvising skills.
    2. Teams will be given a task which has to be completed within the allocated time period.
    3. Points would be deducted in case of time delay of failure of the device/mechanism.
    4. Other rules will be specified during the competition.

      Part 3

    1. The problem statement of the final task will be specified at the commencement of the final round. The finalists would be asked to make a machine as per the problem statement given.
    2. It will also contain a list of machine parts and other components available in the junkyard. If needed the teams will be allowed to visit the junkyard site to check what components can be of their use.
    3. The teams will also be given a certain amount of virtual money. Each and every part available in the junkyard will cost a certain amount of money.
    4. The teams then have to give a rough estimate of the list of components they will require and will have to ensure that the total cost of all the components falls within the virtual money provided to them.
    5. The teams then have to submit their respective design and its specifications to the organizers.
    6. They will have to build the machine they designed by buying the required components from the junkyard.
    7. The making of the machine will involve tasks like welding, building electronic connections, carpentry work etc. For such works help can be provided if needed. (E.g.: Welding of rods)

        8. Final Showdown

      1. After all the teams have built their machines, there will be a head-on competition between all the machines.
      2. The machine which excels this round will be declared as the winner. The winning criteria would be specified during the showdown.
      3. No part of prototype should consist of foreign element, i.e. the one outside the domain of the junk provided.
      4. It is recommended that you keep the physical exertion of the final round in mind while choosing your team mates. Teams cannot be changed between rounds.
      5. Participants need to have a basic idea about operational precautions.
      6. In case of any misconduct, decision of the event organizers shall be final and binding.
    8. All decisions of the judges/ are final and binding.
