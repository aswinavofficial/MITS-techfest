Panchamantra


Think and come out with the most simplest and innovative ideas to solve the given problems in each level of this bumpy journey. Problems need you to apply elementary scientific knowledge, common sense and your ‘out of the box thinking’ ... It gets better and better with each increasing level.



Format

  1. Prelims will be written round in which maximum of 20 teams will be selected for the finals.
  2. The race takes you through 5 different tasks based on the application of engineering, science, logic or mathematics etc.
  3. Teams will have to complete a task fully.
  4. Choose your team wisely... diversity in skill matters. Problems need elementary knowledge of all streams...

Prize Money

₹ 17,500

x

Panchamantra



Rules

  1. The event is open to all college students with valid ID cards.
  2. A team can have a maximum of 3 members.
  3. A person can be part of only one team
  4. The members of the teams can be from different colleges. “If you think you can put science into practice, you are eligible”.
  5. Any valid solutions to the given task will be considered.
  6. Evaluation will be based on the efficiency of your model, your explanation to the solution and how fast you reach the solution.
  7. The most innovative and practically viable solution will help score higher.
  8. The team must complete the round in stipulated time to qualify to next round.
  9. The next task will be given only on the completion of the first task.
  10. The decision of the event organizers will be final and binding under all circumstances.
  11. Participation in malpractices like taking help from the other groups, if caught will be eliminated or disqualified.
  12. If you still have any doubts regarding the rules, please contact the organisers without hesitation.

How Adam Did It


“All of the biggest technological inventions created by man - the airplane, the automobile, the computer - says little about his intelligence, but speaks volumes about his laziness.”-Mark Kennedy

Ever wondered if you were born back then even you too would have came up with some great inventions. This is your chance to put yourself to test and to show your prowess in designing and cracking problems.



Format

  1. There will be two rounds.

      2. Preliminary round:

    1. A written round consisting of 10 questions and 2 separate bonus questions (no tie breaker).

      Second Round

    1. Few teams will be selected for the final.
    2. They will be provided with resources and a problem statement.
    3. The finalists have to make an invention to solve the problem.
    4. In case of a tie, an extra clause may be added to the problem statement by the organizers.

Prize Money

₹ 8,000

x

How Adam Did It



Rules

  1. One team should consist of maximum two persons.
  2. Use of unfair means is strictly prohibited and this will lead to disqualification of the team from the event.


Judgement

  1. The answers of the first round will be limited.
  2. The design problem will be evaluated based on its effectiveness in realizing the problem statement.
  3. For the second round, the score will depend on how closely the design meets the required criteria. The participant should keep in mind, the era specified in the problem. If they have used a material which is ahead of that time, no points will be awarded.
  4. The result of the second round will also depend on the performance of their invention and not on the design. The invention which will perform the best, wins.
  5. The decision of the organisers will be final.
